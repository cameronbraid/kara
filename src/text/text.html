Chapter 1


To anyone who didn’t know better, Riley Miller was a perfectly ordinary fourteen-year-old boy. He was tall and thin with startling green eyes that peered from beneath a forelock of thick black hair. He was a quiet boy, someone who preferred to hang about at the back of a group and who hated being the centre of attention. He had no particular friends at school and liked it that way.
       But in truth, Riley was the opposite of ordinary. Since the age of three, when his parents had been killed in a house fire, he had lived with his draconian uncle, a man who hated him and didn’t bother to hide it. In an effort to find excuses to stay out of the house, Riley took up martial arts lessons, attending three times a week, and he spent all his other afternoons taking extra language lessons – something he had found he enjoyed very much. This is why, by the age of fourteen, Riley Miller was fluent in five languages (Italian, Spanish, German, French and Russian) and was a second Dan black belt in Taekwondo.
       As Riley walked to school one Tuesday morning, he was deep in thought about that day’s chemistry class. Chemistry was his second favourite subject, after languages. He loved learning what elements substances were made of, and how they reacted together. This term his class had be learning about dangerous household chemicals, and Riley couldn’t believe how any of them were legal.
       Lost in his thoughts, Riley ran straight into a brick wall. At least, he thought it was a brick wall until he looked up. A scowling, burly policeman was blocking his way.
       Burly is an understatement, Riley thought, This guy’s a man-mountain!
       “Sorry!” Riley said, embarrassed – and slightly nervous. He tried to duck around the policeman’s muscled torso, but an arm like a tree trunk swung out and a hand the size of a dinner plate landed on his shoulder.



Chapter 2


The sun fought through the clouds and struggled in vain to pierce the thick forest canopy. Below, the raindrops slid off leaves and plummeted to the ground where a pack of dogs lie. On the outskirts of the pack, dogs sit sentry, watching between the trees for movement. The forest is a dangerous place, especially at night.
